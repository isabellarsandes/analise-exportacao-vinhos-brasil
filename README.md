# Brazil Wine Export Analytics

Business analytics project focused on the Brazilian wine industry, exploring export performance, market dynamics, production behavior, and strategic opportunities for international growth.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project was developed as part of the **FIAP Pós Tech – Data Analytics** postgraduate program.

The challenge simulates the role of a **Data Analytics Expert** in a Brazilian company that exports wine worldwide. The goal is to produce analytical reports for investors and stakeholders, explaining export performance, identifying key destination markets, and evaluating external factors that may influence international sales.

The analysis covers the **last 15 years (2009–2023)**, with a business-oriented approach focused on decision-making, market expansion, and strategic insights.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

A Brazilian wine exporter needs an analytical view of its international performance in order to support discussions with investors and shareholders.

The main business challenge is to understand:

- how much wine Brazil exported over the last 15 years
- which countries received the highest export volumes
- which markets generated the most value
- what external factors may affect export performance
- which countries offer future growth potential for Brazilian wine exports

---

## 🎯 Objective

Analyze Brazilian wine exports over the last 15 years in order to:

- Identify the main destination countries
- Measure export volume (liters) and total export value (USD)
- Understand export trends over time
- Evaluate external factors impacting exports, such as:
  - Climate conditions
  - Demographic indicators
  - Economic indicators
  - Wine ratings and market perception
- Generate strategic insights for business expansion
- Propose data-driven recommendations to improve export performance

---

## 📊 Data Sources

This project combines internal and external data sources, including:

- **Brazilian wine export data** from **Embrapa / VitiBrasil**
- Data on:
  - Export destination countries
  - Export volume
  - Export value in US dollars
- Complementary external datasets used to enrich the analysis:
  - Economic data
  - Demographic data
  - Climate-related information
  - Wine market evaluation / ratings

## 🛠 Methodology

The project followed a data analytics workflow composed of:

1. Data collection from official and complementary sources
2. Data cleaning and preprocessing
3. Data transformation and organization
4. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
5. Trend and market concentration analysis
6. Comparative analysis by destination country
7. Price and export value assessment
8. Production analysis for the Brazilian wine sector
9. Strategic interpretation of results
10. Recommendation of future market opportunities

---

## 📈 Key Business Questions

The analysis was designed to answer questions such as:

- Which countries import the highest volume of Brazilian wine?
- How have exports evolved between 2009 and 2023?
- Which markets are more stable and commercially attractive?
- Is Brazil highly dependent on a small number of export destinations?
- How do volume and price per liter vary across countries?
- What production and climate factors affect the sector?
- Which international markets are still underexplored?
- What actions could strengthen Brazilian wine exports in the medium and long term?

---

## 🔍 Main Insights

Based on the final report, the project identified the following key findings:

- Brazilian wine exports show **high volatility** across the analyzed period
- From **2016 onward**, exports show a more consistent recovery in both volume and value
- **Russia** stands out as the largest destination in accumulated volume, but with low regularity
- **Paraguay** appears as the most stable export market over time
- The **United States** presents lower volume, but stronger relevance in terms of value and price per liter
- Brazilian exports are still **highly concentrated in a few markets**, increasing commercial risk
- Exchange-rate fluctuations are an important factor affecting competitiveness
- Brazilian wine production is strongly affected by climate conditions, with a major drop in 2016
- **Sparkling wines** emerge as one of the most promising segments for future international growth
- Markets such as **United Kingdom, Germany, and China** were identified as underexplored opportunities for expansion

---

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations

The final report highlights several business recommendations, including:

- Prioritize markets that are structurally dependent on wine imports
- Maintain continuous and coordinated international expansion efforts
- Strengthen the recurrence of **Wine of Brazil** promotion initiatives
- Tailor commercial campaigns according to each market profile
- Improve partnerships with international importers and retailers
- Use data monitoring to continuously refine export strategy
- Position Brazilian wine competitively in higher-value and niche segments
